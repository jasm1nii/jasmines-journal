17 august 2023

i’ve been meaning to talk more about this unwelcome change once i have properly settled into my new web host, so here’s an updated recap of my recent departure from neocities. full disclosure - nearly a week has passed since both the website and its associated profile was taken down, and i still don’t know why. i’ve already contacted support, but i’ve yet to receive a response. (oddly enough, i can still receive password reset emails, but none of the links go through.)

all that aside… i’ve since forked out some cash to reupload my website (again) to a more established hosting service under a new domain, but i don’t plan on leaving ichi.city behind.

i miss connecting with people via neocities’ discovery feed, and i genuinely enjoyed the social aspect of the service - sometimes, it feels a bit lonely existing within an isolated pocket of my own. i liked being able to share what i’ve made for myself without persistently promoting myself on other social media platforms or “improving my SEO”, and i don’t feel like venturing very far outside of the smaller, cozier communities that already exist within the indie web.

so with that said, i’ll continue mirroring “jasmine’s journal” to https://jasm1nii.ichi.city/ for as long as i’m able to, or at least until i hit the upper limit of my allotted storage space (10 megabytes, to be exact). for reference, this website takes up a grand total of around 4 MB on my hard drive, but it will likely explode in size once i get my illustration gallery up. while i have more than enough room to accommodate for these planned additions on my paid hosting service, i can’t guarantee that i’ll reupload everything to ichi, but i’ll try to work around the limit by using fallback links or uploading my images to dropbox instead.
