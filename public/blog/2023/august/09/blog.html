detour down memory lane - opens on a tangent related to coding, but is mostly about school (2 paragraphs)

okay, going off the rails for a bit since this just popped into my head - i actually thought about majoring in computer science early in my high school years. but to my disadvantage, my grades for math and other prerequisite subjects seriously sucked. soon enough, i came to terms with reality and refocused my efforts towards applying to various art and design programs - and it worked out in the end...! until i hit burnout, that is. however, i'd like to note that, despite being truly passionate about the field, not feeling like i had any other option back then because of my poor academic performance was still a major deciding factor. (thanks, undiagnosed neurodivergencies.)

it wasn't always like this; if anything, school used to be a breeze before i moved back to my country of birth. i'll acknowledge that, at the time, my incompetence in the language definitely played a part in exacerbating my negative experiences with the indonesian education system, but i was also subjected to a lot of unsavory experiences - perhaps one day, i'll talk about this in a longer post of its own.
